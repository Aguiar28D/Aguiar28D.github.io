About me

Hi! I'm Diego

Economist turned data scientist — bridging rigorous analysis with practical ML and business impact.

Based in Montevideo, Uruguay, I'm an economist who moved into data science through academic research, teaching, and now industry. I spent years doing causal inference research and teaching Macroeconomics before moving into industry, where I now build fraud prevention and analytics systems at Mercado Libre. I hold two master's degrees from Arizona State University and the University of Georgia, and a bachelor's from Universidad Católica del Uruguay. I apply ML and AI hands-on through a postgraduate specialization at UTEC + MIT, and I've taught university-level Macroeconomics, Calculus, Statistics, and Applied Data Science.

Used Car Search Chatbot with RAG

Car buyers struggle to describe what they want in search filters. This chatbot lets them search 38,531 used vehicle listings through natural conversation — asking for "an affordable family SUV under $15K" and getting relevant results, with follow-up questions understood in context. Built with LangChain, FAISS vector search, and GPT-4o-mini. Final project for the Deep Learning module (UTEC + MIT).

RoastAI: Computer Vision for Coffee Roast Classification

Coffee roast classification is typically done by hand — slow, subjective, and hard to scale. This project automates it with a ResNet18 computer vision model achieving 85%+ accuracy, with Grad-CAM to explain which visual features drive each prediction. Paired with 60+ years of global production data to put roast trends in business context.

PyTorch ResNet18 Grad-CAM Python

House Price Prediction in Uruguay

Real estate pricing in Uruguay is opaque — sellers and buyers have little data to anchor expectations. This project scrapes listings from Mercado Libre and El Gallito (2023–2025), builds a CatBoost model tuned with Optuna, and achieves ~25% MAPE for Montevideo. Interpretability analysis reveals which neighborhoods and features drive price the most.
